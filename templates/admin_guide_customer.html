Welcome to the Work-A-Beez Administrator Guide.

As an Administrator, you have access to powerful tools that allow you to manage your workforce, configure company settings, assign permissions, monitor employee activity, and maintain compliance.

Administrator Responsibilities

Administrators are responsible for configuring and maintaining the Work-A-Beez platform for their organization.

Typical responsibilities include:

  • Managing employee accounts
  • Creating departments
  • Configuring locations
  • Defining work schedules
  • Managing leave policies
  • Reviewing timecards
  • Managing user permissions
  • Running payroll reports
  • Monitoring attendance
  • Publishing announcements
  • Managing company documents

Best Practices

We recommend:

  • Reviewing permissions quarterly
  • Removing inactive users promptly
  • Using standardized job titles

  • Enabling Multi-Factor Authentication

  • Assigning at least two Company Administrators

Following these practices helps maintain a secure and well-organized workforce management environment.

Roles & Permissions

Work-A-Beez uses Role-Based Access Control (RBAC) to ensure users only have access to the features and information necessary for their responsibilities.

Permissions are assigned through roles and may be customized by Company Administrators.

Default Roles

The platform includes the following default roles.

System Administrator

Typically reserved for Work-A-Beez personnel.

Capabilities include:

  • Platform Administration
  • Tenant Management
  • Platform Monitoring
  • Customer Support
  • Security Administration
  • Platform Configuration

This role is not available to customers.

Company Administrator

Highest permission level available to customer organizations.

Capabilities include:

  • Company Settings
  • Employee Management
  • Billing
  • Reports
  • Scheduling
  • Timekeeping Rules
  • Payroll Export
  • Security Settings
  • Department Management
  • Location Management
  • Announcements
  • API Configuration
  • Audit Logs
  • Subscription Management

Human Resources

Designed for HR professionals.

Capabilities include:

  • Employee Profiles
  • Leave Management
  • Onboarding
  • Employee Documents
  • Policies
  • Performance Records
  • Attendance Reports

Cannot:

  • Modify subscription billing
  • Change security settings
  • Delete company records

Payroll Administrator

Responsible for payroll preparation.

Capabilities include:

  • Payroll Export
  • Timecard Review
  • Overtime Review
  • Holiday Hours
  • PTO Review
  • Attendance Reports

Cannot:

  • Manage subscriptions
  • Delete employees

Operations Manager

Capabilities include:

Follow the Principle of Least Privilege.

Users should receive only the permissions necessary to perform their job.

Avoid assigning Company Administrator access unless absolutely necessary.

Administrator Best Practices

We recommend:

  • Review permissions monthly

  • Test notification emails monthly

Troubleshooting

I cannot edit an employee.

Possible causes:

  • Missing permission
  • Read-only role
  • Employee is archived
  • Department restrictions

Schedule cannot be published.

Possible causes:

  • Unassigned shifts
  • Missing locations
  • Department conflicts
  • Overtime rules violated

Employee cannot clock in.

Possible causes:

  • Account disabled
  • Outside geofence
  • Schedule restrictions
  • Device restrictions
  • Network connectivity

Continue to Chapter 3 – Timekeeping Configuration for configuring clock rules, overtime, rounding, meal periods, geofencing, attendance policies, payroll periods, and approval workflows.

Timekeeping Configuration

Timekeeping is one of the core capabilities of Work-A-Beez. Proper configuration ensures accurate payroll processing, compliance with labor regulations, and a consistent employee experience.

Navigate to Administration → Timekeeping.

The Timekeeping Settings page allows administrators to configure company-wide rules that govern how employees record their work hours.

Timekeeping Overview

The Timekeeping module controls:

  • Clock In rules
  • Clock Out rules
  • Breaks and Meal Periods
  • Overtime calculations
  • Shift differentials
  • Rounding rules
  • Attendance policies
  • Geofencing
  • Geolocation requirements
  • Device restrictions
  • Payroll periods
  • Approval workflows

Changes to these settings affect all users unless overridden by department or location policies.

Clock In Settings

Administrators can define when employees are permitted to clock in.

Configuration options include:

  • Allow clock-in before scheduled shift
  • Prevent early clock-ins
  • Require scheduled shift
  • Allow unscheduled clock-ins
  • Require manager approval for exceptions

Example:

Employees may clock in up to 15 minutes before their scheduled shift.

If an employee attempts to clock in earlier than the configured window, Work-A-Beez displays an informative message and records the attempt in the audit log.

Clock Out Settings

Configure when employees may end their shifts.

Available options include:

  • Prevent early clock-out
  • Allow overtime clock-out
  • Require manager approval
  • Automatic shift completion reminders
  • Missing clock-out notifications

Administrators may configure automatic alerts when an employee remains clocked in beyond a specified duration.

Shift Grace Periods

Grace periods help reduce unnecessary attendance exceptions.

Typical configuration includes:

  • Early Arrival Grace Period
  • Late Arrival Grace Period
  • Early Departure Grace Period
  • Late Departure Grace Period

Example:

Setting Value
Early Clock-In 15 minutes
Late Arrival 5 minutes
Early Departure 5 minutes
Late Clock-Out Unlimited

Attendance reports reflect configured grace periods when calculating tardiness.

Time Rounding Rules

Organizations may choose to round recorded punches.

Supported options include:

  • No rounding
  • Nearest 1 minute
  • Nearest 5 minutes
  • Nearest 6 minutes
  • Nearest 10 minutes
  • Nearest 15 minutes

Administrators should ensure rounding practices comply with applicable labor laws.

Automatic Meal Periods

Meal periods may be:

  • Automatically deducted
  • Employee initiated
  • Supervisor approved
  • Required after a specified number of hours

Configuration options include:

  • Meal duration
  • Minimum shift length
  • Paid meal
  • Unpaid meal
  • Multiple meal periods
  • Automatic deduction exceptions

Example:

Employees working more than six consecutive hours receive a 30-minute unpaid meal deduction unless a supervisor removes the deduction based on documented business needs.

Rest Break Configuration

Organizations may configure paid or unpaid rest breaks.

Settings include:

  • Break duration
  • Number of breaks
  • Paid status
  • Eligibility
  • Reminder notifications

Break policies can vary by department or location where permitted.

Overtime Rules

Administrators may configure overtime according to organizational policy and applicable law.

Common configurations include:

  • Daily overtime
  • Weekly overtime
  • Double time
  • Holiday overtime
  • Seventh consecutive day rules

Supported calculation methods include:

  • Time-and-a-half
  • Double time
  • Custom multipliers

Example:

  • Overtime begins after 40 hours worked in a workweek.
  • Double time applies after 12 hours worked in a single day (where applicable).

Shift Differentials

Organizations may compensate employees differently based on shift characteristics.

Examples include:

  • Evening Shift Premium
  • Overnight Shift Premium
  • Weekend Differential
  • Holiday Premium
  • Hazard Pay

Shift differential rules may be based on:

  • Time of day
  • Department
  • Location
  • Job title
  • Employee classification

Attendance Rules

Attendance policies determine how Work-A-Beez classifies employee attendance.

Configurable events include:

  • Late Arrival
  • Early Departure
  • Missed Punch
  • No Call / No Show
  • Unscheduled Absence
  • Excused Absence
  • Unexcused Absence

Administrators may define threshold values for each attendance event.

Missed Punch Management

Employees who forget to clock in or out may submit a correction request.

Correction requests typically include:

  • Date
  • Time
  • Reason
  • Comments
  • Supporting documentation (optional)

Supervisors or administrators may:

  • Approve
  • Reject
  • Request additional information

All approved corrections are permanently recorded in the audit history.

Payroll Workweek

Configure the organization's payroll workweek.

Settings include:

  • Workweek start day
  • Payroll period

  • Payroll export schedule

Supported payroll frequencies include:

  • Weekly

  • Semi-monthly
  • Monthly

Automatic Clock-Out

Administrators may enable automatic clock-out to prevent excessive recorded work hours.

Configuration options include:

  • Maximum shift duration
  • Automatic reminders
  • Manager notifications
  • Automatic clock-out time

Example:

Employees remaining clocked in for more than 16 consecutive hours are automatically clocked out and flagged for administrative review.

Compliance Notifications

Examples include:

  • Missed meal periods

  • Unauthorized early clock-ins
  • Missed punches
  • Attendance threshold violations

Notifications may be delivered through:

  • Dashboard alerts
  • Email
  • Mobile push notifications
  • In-app notifications

Timekeeping Audit Log

Examples include:

  • Qualification Matching

Shift Conflicts

Work-A-Beez automatically detects scheduling conflicts.

Examples include:

  • Double Booking
  • Overtime Risk
  • Overlapping Shifts
  • Employee Unavailable
  • Insufficient Rest Between Shifts
  • Location Conflict
  • Certification Expired

Employees may submit their preferred working availability.

Availability may include:

  • Preferred Days
  • Preferred Hours
  • Maximum Weekly Hours
  • Unavailable Dates
  • Temporary Restrictions
  • Permanent Restrictions

Administrators may use availability as a scheduling recommendation or enforce it as a scheduling requirement.

Shift Swaps

Employees may request to exchange scheduled shifts with eligible coworkers.

The Shift Swap process typically includes:

  1. Employee submits a request.
  2. Eligible employees may accept the request.
  3. Supervisor reviews the proposed exchange.
  4. Administrator or Supervisor approves or denies the request.
  5. The schedule updates automatically after approval.

All approved changes are recorded in the audit log.

Schedule Publishing

Schedules remain in Draft status until published.

Publishing a schedule:

  • Makes shifts visible to employees.
  • Sends notifications to affected employees.
  • Updates employee calendars.
  • Enables clock-in eligibility based on scheduled shifts.

Administrators may publish:

  • Individual Shifts
  • Departments
  • Locations
  • Entire Schedules

Scheduling Best Practices

To maximize scheduling efficiency, Work-A-Beez recommends:

  • Publish schedules at least one week in advance.
  • Review employee availability before assigning shifts.
  • Monitor overtime projections during schedule creation.
  • Use recurring shift templates whenever possible.
  • Resolve scheduling conflicts before publishing.
  • Maintain current employee certifications and qualifications.
  • Review labor cost projections prior to final approval.
  • Encourage employees to keep availability up to date.
  • Use open shifts to fill staffing shortages before assigning overtime.
  • Regularly review schedule audit logs for accuracy and accountability.
