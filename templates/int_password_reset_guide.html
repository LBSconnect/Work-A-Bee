Welcome to the Work-A-Beez Password Reset Guide.

This guide explains how users can securely reset a forgotten password, how administrators can assist with account recovery, and how to resolve common password reset issues.

Password recovery is designed to protect your account while providing a simple and secure method for regaining access.

Overview

If you are unable to access your Work-A-Beez account because you forgot your password or believe it has been compromised, you can reset it using the secure password recovery process.

Depending on your organization's security configuration, password resets may be completed:

  • Through self-service recovery
  • By an administrator
  • By your IT department
  • Through your organization's Identity Provider (IdP) when Single Sign-On (SSO) is enabled

When to Reset Your Password

You should reset your password if:

  • You forgot your password.
  • You suspect your password has been compromised.
  • Your administrator requires a password change.
  • Your password has expired.
  • Your account has been secured following suspicious activity.

Do not continue attempting to log in repeatedly if you are unsure of your password, as this may trigger an account lockout.

Self-Service Password Reset

If your organization has enabled self-service password reset, follow these steps:

  1. Navigate to the Work-A-Beez sign-in page.
  2. Select Forgot Password?
  3. Enter your registered email address or username.
  4. Select Continue.
  5. Complete any required identity verification.
  6. Retrieve the password reset email or verification code.
  7. Open the secure password reset link.
  8. Create a new password that meets your organization's password requirements.
  9. Confirm the new password.
  10. Sign in using your updated credentials.

If successful, you will receive confirmation that your password has been changed.

Administrator-Initiated Password Reset

If self-service password reset is unavailable, contact your Work-A-Beez administrator.

An administrator may:

  • Verify your identity.
  • Initiate a password reset.
  • Issue a temporary password (if configured).
  • Require a password change at your next sign-in.

Administrators should never request your current password.

Identity Verification

Before a password reset can be completed, your identity may need to be verified.

Verification methods may include:

  • Email verification
  • One-time verification code
  • Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)
  • Security questions (if enabled)
  • Identity verification by an administrator

Verification requirements vary based on your organization's security policies.

Password Requirements

Your new password must comply with your organization's Password Policy.

Typical requirements include:

  • Minimum length
  • Maximum length
  • Uppercase letters
  • Lowercase letters
  • Numbers
  • Special characters

Your new password should also:

  • Be unique.
  • Not match recently used passwords.
  • Avoid personal information.
  • Avoid common or easily guessed words.

For complete requirements, refer to the Password Policy.

Expired Reset Links

Password reset links are intentionally short-lived for security purposes.

A reset link may expire if:

  • It has already been used.
  • The expiration period has passed.
  • A newer password reset request has been submitted.
  • The administrator invalidated the request.

If your link has expired:

  1. Return to the sign-in page.
  2. Select Forgot Password?
  3. Request a new password reset email.
  4. Use the most recent reset link provided.

Locked Accounts

Your account may become temporarily locked after multiple unsuccessful login attempts.

If your account is locked:

  • Wait for the configured lockout period to expire, or
  • Contact your administrator if manual account unlock is required.

Do not continue attempting to sign in while the account is locked.

Multi-Factor Authentication During Password Reset

If Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) is enabled, additional verification may be required during the password reset process.

Examples include:

  • Authenticator application approval
  • SMS verification code
  • Email verification code
  • Hardware security key
  • Push notification approval

MFA helps ensure only authorized users can reset passwords.

Common Troubleshooting

I Didn't Receive the Password Reset Email

Check:

  • Spam or Junk folders
  • Email filtering rules
  • Correct email address
  • Corporate email quarantine

If the email still does not arrive, contact your administrator.

My Reset Link Doesn't Work

Possible reasons include:

  • The link has expired.
  • The link has already been used.
  • A newer reset request was generated.
  • The URL was copied incorrectly.

Request a new password reset link and use the most recent email.

My New Password Is Rejected

Verify that your password:

  • Meets complexity requirements.
  • Does not match recently used passwords.
  • Does not contain prohibited words or personal information.
  • Meets your organization's minimum length requirements.

I Am Still Locked Out

If you continue to experience issues:

  • Confirm your username or email address.
  • Verify that MFA is functioning correctly.
  • Contact your administrator or the Work-A-Beez Support Center.

I Use Single Sign-On (SSO)

If your organization authenticates users through an external Identity Provider, you must reset your password through your organization's identity management system rather than within Work-A-Beez.

Contact your IT department if you are unsure.

Security Best Practices

To keep your account secure:

  • Use a unique password for Work-A-Beez.
  • Never share your password.
  • Enable Multi-Factor Authentication whenever available.
  • Use a trusted password manager.
  • Reset your password immediately if you suspect compromise.
  • Sign out when using shared devices.
  • Avoid saving passwords on public or shared computers.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long is a password reset link valid?

Reset links expire automatically after a configurable period for security reasons. If the link expires, request a new one.

Can I reuse my old password?

No.

Your organization may prevent reuse of recently used passwords according to its Password Policy.

Why do I need to verify my identity?

Identity verification helps prevent unauthorized individuals from gaining access to your account.

Can an administrator see my password?

No.

Administrators cannot view your current password. They can only initiate a secure password reset process.

What should I do if I believe someone else reset my password?

Immediately:

  1. Contact your administrator.
  2. Notify the Work-A-Beez Support Center.
  3. Change your password if you still have access.
  4. Review your recent account activity if available.
