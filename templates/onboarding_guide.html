Introduction

A successful implementation begins with careful planning.

Most organizations complete their initial setup in a few hours, although larger organizations with multiple locations, complex scheduling requirements, or custom policies may require additional time.

This guide follows the recommended implementation sequence developed by the Work-A-Beez Customer Success team.

Before You Begin

Before configuring your account, gather the following information:

Company Information

  • Legal Company Name
  • Business Address
  • Company Phone Number
  • Website
  • Company Logo
  • Time Zone
  • Payroll Schedule

Workforce Information

  • Employee List
  • Departments
  • Job Titles
  • Managers
  • Locations
  • Work Schedules

Policies

  • PTO Policy
  • Attendance Policy
  • Break Policy
  • Overtime Policy
  • Holiday Calendar
  • Leave Rules

Having this information available before beginning setup will significantly reduce implementation time.

Creating Your Organization

Your Work-A-Beez subscription includes a dedicated organization, sometimes referred to as a tenant.

Your organization provides:

  • Secure data isolation
  • Organization-specific settings
  • Employee directory
  • Schedules
  • Reports
  • Payroll data
  • Permissions
  • Branding

Each organization operates independently from every other Work-A-Beez customer.

Administrator Account Setup

The first account created during implementation becomes the Primary Company Administrator.

The Primary Administrator is responsible for:

  • Completing initial setup
  • Inviting additional administrators
  • Managing subscriptions
  • Configuring security settings
  • Assigning permissions

For business continuity, Work-A-Beez recommends assigning at least one additional Company Administrator.

Company Profile

Navigate to Administration → Company Settings.

Complete the following information:

  • Company Name
  • Business Address
  • Phone Number
  • Website
  • Primary Contact
  • Time Zone
  • Default Language
  • Currency
  • Date Format
  • Time Format

Accurate company information ensures reports, notifications, and exported documents display the correct organizational details.

Business Locations

Organizations with multiple offices, stores, branches, or facilities should create each location before importing employees.

For each location, configure:

  • Location Name
  • Address
  • Phone Number
  • Time Zone
  • Manager
  • Operating Hours
  • GPS Coordinates (optional)
  • Geofence Radius (optional)

Locations may have independent schedules, departments, and labor rules.

Departments

Departments organize employees into logical business units.

Common examples include:

  • Human Resources
  • Operations
  • Customer Service
  • Sales
  • Accounting
  • Information Technology
  • Warehouse
  • Maintenance
  • Administration

Departments improve reporting, scheduling, and permission management.

Job Titles

Create standardized job titles before importing employees.

Examples:

  • Operations Manager
  • Supervisor
  • Customer Service Representative
  • Cashier
  • Technician
  • Driver
  • Administrative Assistant

Consistent job titles improve reporting accuracy and simplify workforce planning.

Roles & Permissions

Assign appropriate security roles before inviting users.

Typical roles include:

  • Company Administrator
  • Human Resources
  • Payroll Administrator
  • Operations Manager
  • Supervisor
  • Employee

Follow the Principle of Least Privilege by granting users only the permissions required to perform their responsibilities.

Importing Employees

Employees may be added individually or imported in bulk using a CSV file.

Typical employee data includes:

  • First Name
  • Last Name
  • Email Address
  • Employee ID
  • Department
  • Job Title
  • Manager
  • Location
  • Hire Date
  • Employment Status

After import, invitation emails can be sent to employees automatically or at a later time.

Timekeeping Configuration

Configure organization-wide timekeeping policies before employees begin recording time.

Review:

  • Clock-In Rules
  • Clock-Out Rules
  • Break Policies
  • Meal Periods
  • Overtime Rules
  • Rounding Rules
  • Payroll Workweek
  • Attendance Policies

These settings should align with your organization's policies and applicable labor regulations.

Scheduling Configuration

If your organization uses scheduling, configure:

  • Shift Templates
  • Business Hours
  • Recurring Shifts
  • Departments
  • Locations
  • Scheduling Rules
  • Open Shift Policies

Publishing a sample schedule before launch is recommended.

Leave Policies

Configure all applicable leave types.

Examples include:

  • Vacation
  • Sick Leave
  • Personal Leave
  • Bereavement Leave
  • Jury Duty
  • Military Leave
  • Family and Medical Leave (FMLA)

Define accrual methods, carryover rules, approval requirements, and eligibility criteria.

Company Holidays

Create your organization's holiday calendar.

For each holiday, specify:

  • Holiday Name
  • Date
  • Paid or Unpaid
  • Premium Pay Rules
  • Required Staffing (if applicable)

Holiday settings integrate with scheduling and payroll calculations.

Notifications

Review notification settings to ensure administrators and employees receive timely updates.

Available notifications include:

  • Schedule Published
  • Shift Assigned
  • Shift Swap Request
  • PTO Request
  • Approval Required
  • Missed Punch
  • Overtime Alert
  • Company Announcement

Notifications may be delivered by email, mobile push, SMS (if enabled), or in-app messaging.

Branding

Customize the platform to reflect your organization's identity.

Branding options include:

  • Company Logo
  • Accent Colors
  • Welcome Message
  • Login Screen Background
  • Email Header

Branding provides employees with a familiar and professional experience.

Security Configuration

Before launch, review the following security settings:

  • Password Requirements
  • Multi-Factor Authentication
  • Session Timeout
  • IP Restrictions (if applicable)
  • Device Policies
  • Administrator Permissions

Security should be reviewed periodically as your organization grows.

Testing Your Configuration

Before inviting employees, verify your setup by completing the following:

  • Create a test employee account.
  • Clock in and out.
  • Submit a PTO request.
  • Publish a sample schedule.
  • Approve a request.
  • Generate a payroll report.
  • Verify notification delivery.
  • Confirm role permissions.

Testing helps identify configuration issues before production use.

Go-Live Checklist

Before launching Work-A-Beez organization-wide, verify that:

  • Company information is complete.
  • Locations have been created.
  • Departments have been configured.
  • Job titles are standardized.
  • Employee records have been imported.
  • Timekeeping rules have been tested.
  • Scheduling rules have been reviewed.
  • Leave policies have been configured.
  • Security settings have been verified.
  • At least two Company Administrators have been assigned.
  • Notifications are functioning correctly.
  • Branding has been applied.

After Go-Live

Following implementation, administrators should:

  • Monitor employee adoption.
  • Review audit logs.
  • Resolve support requests promptly.
  • Review attendance reports.
  • Monitor overtime trends.
  • Conduct periodic permission reviews.
  • Update company policies as needed.

Continuous review helps ensure the platform continues to meet organizational needs.

Getting Support

If you require assistance during implementation or after launch, Work-A-Beez offers several support options.

Available resources include:

  • Online Documentation
  • Knowledge Base
  • Video Tutorials
  • Customer Support
  • Product Release Notes

Support contact information can be found within your organization's support portal or on the Work-A-Beez website.
