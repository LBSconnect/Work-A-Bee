Welcome to the Work-A-Beez Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) Guide.

Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) provides an additional layer of security beyond a username and password by requiring users to verify their identity using a second authentication factor.

This guide explains how MFA works, how to enroll, supported authentication methods, and how to manage and troubleshoot MFA for your Work-A-Beez account.

Overview

Multi-Factor Authentication requires users to verify their identity using two or more authentication factors.

Authentication factors typically include:

  • Something you know (password)
  • Something you have (mobile device or security key)
  • Something you are (biometric authentication where supported)

Even if a password is compromised, MFA significantly reduces the likelihood of unauthorized account access.

Why MFA Matters

MFA helps protect against:

  • Stolen passwords
  • Phishing attacks
  • Credential stuffing
  • Password reuse
  • Brute-force attacks
  • Unauthorized remote access

Organizations are strongly encouraged to enable MFA for all users, particularly those with administrative privileges.

How MFA Works

After entering a valid username and password, users complete a second verification step before access is granted.

Typical sign-in process:

  1. Enter username.
  2. Enter password.
  3. Complete second-factor verification.
  4. Access Work-A-Beez.

If verification fails, access is denied.

Supported Authentication Methods

Depending on your organization's configuration, Work-A-Beez may support one or more of the following authentication methods.

Authenticator Applications

Authenticator apps generate time-based one-time passwords (TOTP).

Examples include:

  • Microsoft Authenticator
  • Google Authenticator
  • Authy
  • Other compatible TOTP applications

Push Notifications

Users receive a notification on a registered mobile device and approve or deny the sign-in request.

SMS Verification Codes

A one-time verification code is sent to a registered mobile phone.

SMS should be used only when stronger authentication methods are unavailable.

Email Verification Codes

A one-time code is sent to the user's registered email address.

Hardware Security Keys

Organizations may use FIDO2 or WebAuthn-compatible security keys for enhanced security.

Examples include USB, NFC, or Bluetooth security keys.

Biometric Authentication

Where supported by the user's device and authentication provider, biometric authentication may be used.

Examples include:

  • Fingerprint recognition
  • Facial recognition

Biometric information remains managed by the device or identity provider and is not stored by Work-A-Beez.

Enrolling in MFA

Users may be prompted to enroll in MFA during their first sign-in or when required by their organization.

Typical enrollment process:

  1. Sign in to Work-A-Beez.
  2. Navigate to Account Settings.
  3. Select Security.
  4. Choose Multi-Factor Authentication.
  5. Select an authentication method.
  6. Follow the enrollment instructions.
  7. Verify the authentication method.
  8. Save your changes.

Enrollment is complete after successful verification.

Signing In with MFA

During sign-in:

  1. Enter your username.
  2. Enter your password.
  3. Complete the requested verification step.
  4. Access your account.

Depending on organizational policy, trusted devices may reduce the frequency of MFA prompts.

Managing Authentication Methods

Users may manage their authentication methods from the Security settings.

Available actions may include:

  • Add a new authenticator
  • Remove an old device
  • Change the default authentication method
  • Register additional devices
  • Update recovery information

Organizations may restrict some changes based on security policy.

Backup Authentication Methods

Users are encouraged to register more than one authentication method.

Examples include:

  • Authenticator app
  • Hardware security key
  • Secondary mobile device
  • Backup email (if permitted)

Having multiple authentication methods helps prevent account lockouts.

Lost or Replaced Devices

If your authentication device is lost, stolen, or replaced:

  1. Contact your administrator if you cannot sign in.
  2. Verify your identity.
  3. Remove the lost device from your account.
  4. Register your replacement device.
  5. Review recent account activity.

If you believe your device has been compromised, report it immediately.

Recovery Process

If you cannot complete MFA verification:

  • Use another registered authentication method.
  • Use recovery codes if your organization provides them.
  • Contact your administrator.
  • Contact the Work-A-Beez Support Center if additional assistance is required.

Recovery procedures vary depending on your organization's authentication configuration.

Administrator Controls

Administrators may configure:

  • MFA enforcement
  • Allowed authentication methods
  • Trusted device policies
  • Session duration
  • Recovery procedures
  • Administrative reset of MFA enrollment
  • Conditional access requirements

Administrative changes should align with the organization's security policies and regulatory requirements.

Security Best Practices

To maximize account security:

  • Enable MFA for all users.
  • Prefer authenticator apps or hardware security keys over SMS when possible.
  • Register multiple authentication methods.
  • Keep recovery information up to date.
  • Never approve unexpected MFA prompts.
  • Report suspicious authentication requests immediately.
  • Remove old or unused devices from your account.
  • Secure your mobile devices with screen locks or biometrics.

Troubleshooting

I Didn't Receive My Verification Code

Verify:

  • Cellular service (for SMS)
  • Internet connectivity (for authenticator apps)
  • Email spam folder (for email verification)
  • Correct registered phone number or email address

My Authenticator Code Is Incorrect

Possible causes include:

  • Incorrect device time
  • Expired one-time password
  • Incorrect account selected in the authenticator app

Ensure your device's date and time are synchronized automatically.

I Replaced My Phone

If you cannot access your previous authenticator:

  • Use another registered authentication method.
  • Contact your administrator.
  • Complete identity verification.
  • Register your new device.

I Accidentally Denied an MFA Prompt

Simply attempt to sign in again.

If repeated unexpected prompts occur, change your password immediately and notify your administrator, as this may indicate attempted unauthorized access.

My Security Key Is Not Working

Check:

  • Browser compatibility
  • USB or NFC connection
  • Supported browser version
  • Security key registration

Try another supported browser if necessary.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is MFA required?

Organizations determine whether MFA is mandatory. Work-A-Beez strongly recommends enabling MFA for all users.

Which authentication method is the most secure?

Hardware security keys and authenticator applications generally provide stronger protection than SMS verification.

Can I register multiple devices?

Yes.

Organizations may allow users to register multiple authentication methods or devices for redundancy.

What happens if I lose my phone?

Use another registered authentication method or contact your administrator to complete the recovery process.

Can administrators disable MFA?

Administrators may reset or modify MFA enrollment according to organizational policies. They cannot bypass identity verification requirements established by the authentication system.
