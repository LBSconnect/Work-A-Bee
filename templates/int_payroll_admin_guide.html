Welcome to the Work-A-Beez Payroll Administration Guide.

Payroll is one of the most critical functions within the Work-A-Beez platform. This guide explains how to configure payroll settings, manage pay groups and pay periods, approve employee timecards, calculate payable hours, export payroll data, and maintain payroll accuracy.

While Work-A-Beez is not a payroll processing system, it serves as the authoritative source for employee work hours and attendance, ensuring payroll systems receive accurate, validated data.

Overview

The Payroll module enables organizations to:

  • Collect approved work hours
  • Calculate payable time
  • Apply overtime rules
  • Include approved leave
  • Validate employee timecards
  • Export payroll-ready data
  • Maintain audit history

Payroll administrators should complete all validations before exporting payroll data.

Payroll Architecture

Payroll information is generated from several platform components:

  • Employee Profiles
  • Time Entries
  • Break Records
  • Schedules
  • Approved Leave
  • Overtime Rules
  • Holiday Calendars
  • Pay Groups
  • Payroll Periods

These components work together to produce accurate payroll data.

Payroll Roles & Permissions

Payroll access is controlled through role-based permissions.

Payroll Administrator

Can:

  • Configure payroll settings
  • Open payroll periods
  • Approve payroll
  • Lock payroll
  • Export payroll
  • View payroll reports

HR Administrator

Can:

  • Review employee records
  • Approve leave
  • Correct employee information

Supervisor

Can:

  • Review employee timecards
  • Approve employee hours
  • Submit corrections

Employee

Can:

  • View personal timecards
  • Submit corrections
  • Review approved hours

Employees cannot modify approved payroll records.

Payroll Configuration

Payroll settings include:

  • Default workweek
  • Payroll frequency
  • Time rounding
  • Overtime calculations
  • Holiday rules
  • PTO calculations
  • Export preferences
  • Default earnings codes

Configuration changes should be reviewed before a payroll period begins.

Pay Groups

Pay Groups organize employees who share common payroll characteristics.

Typical grouping methods include:

  • Weekly payroll
  • Bi-weekly payroll
  • Semi-monthly payroll
  • Monthly payroll

Pay Groups determine:

  • Payroll calendar
  • Approval deadlines
  • Export schedules
  • Payroll administrators

Pay Periods

Each payroll cycle consists of:

  • Period Start Date
  • Period End Date
  • Approval Deadline
  • Export Date
  • Payroll Status

Typical statuses include:

  • Open
  • Under Review
  • Pending Approval
  • Approved
  • Locked
  • Exported

Earnings Codes

Earnings codes categorize payable hours.

Examples include:

Code Description
REG Regular Hours
OT Overtime
DT Double Time
HOL Holiday Pay
PTO Paid Time Off
SICK Sick Leave
VAC Vacation
TRN Training

Organizations may create additional earnings codes based on business requirements.

Overtime Rules

Overtime calculations may be configured according to organizational policies and applicable labor laws.

Examples include:

  • Daily overtime
  • Weekly overtime
  • Double time
  • Consecutive day overtime
  • Shift differential
  • Premium pay

Administrators should validate overtime configurations before processing payroll.

Holiday Pay

Holiday settings define:

  • Recognized holidays
  • Holiday schedules
  • Eligibility requirements
  • Premium pay rates
  • Substitute holidays

Holiday rules are automatically applied to eligible employees based on organizational policy.

PTO & Leave Calculations

Approved leave may contribute to payroll calculations.

Supported leave types include:

  • Vacation
  • Sick Leave
  • Personal Leave
  • Floating Holidays
  • Jury Duty
  • Bereavement
  • Military Leave

Accrual balances are updated according to configured organizational policies.

Timecard Review

Before payroll approval, administrators should review:

  • Missing punches
  • Overtime
  • Break violations
  • Attendance exceptions
  • Approved corrections
  • Leave approvals
  • Shift premiums

Timecards requiring attention should be corrected before approval.

Payroll Approval Workflow

Typical payroll workflow:

  1. Employees submit time.
  2. Supervisors review timecards.
  3. Supervisors approve hours.
  4. Payroll reviews exceptions.
  5. Payroll administrator approves payroll.
  6. Payroll is locked.
  7. Payroll is exported.

Each approval action is recorded in the audit log.

Payroll Locking

Once payroll is approved, administrators may lock the payroll period.

Locking prevents:

  • Timecard edits
  • Schedule changes
  • Payroll recalculation
  • Additional approvals

Unlocking payroll requires administrator authorization.

Payroll Exports

Payroll-ready data can be exported for external payroll processing.

Supported export formats include:

  • CSV
  • Excel (.xlsx)
  • JSON (where supported)

Typical exported fields include:

  • Employee ID
  • Employee Name
  • Earnings Code
  • Hours Worked
  • Overtime
  • Leave Hours
  • Department
  • Location
  • Pay Period

Organizations should validate exported data before importing it into payroll systems.

Payroll Adjustments

Occasionally payroll corrections are required.

Examples include:

  • Missed punches
  • Incorrect job codes
  • Shift premium corrections
  • Overtime adjustments
  • Retroactive pay corrections

Adjustments should be documented with the reason for the change.

Payroll Auditing

Payroll audit reports provide visibility into:

  • Timecard edits
  • Approval history
  • Payroll exports
  • Payroll corrections
  • Administrator actions
  • User activity

Audit history supports compliance and internal controls.

Best Practices

To improve payroll accuracy:

  • Review exceptions daily.
  • Approve timecards before payroll deadlines.
  • Verify overtime calculations.
  • Confirm leave approvals.
  • Lock payroll after approval.
  • Archive payroll reports.
  • Regularly review audit logs.

Consistent payroll procedures reduce errors and improve operational efficiency.

Troubleshooting

Missing Employee Hours

Verify:

  • Employee assigned to correct pay group
  • Approved timecards
  • Payroll period dates
  • Schedule assignments

Incorrect Overtime

Check:

  • Overtime rules
  • Shift assignments
  • Workweek definitions
  • Holiday settings

Export Validation Errors

Review:

  • Missing employee IDs
  • Invalid earnings codes
  • Required fields
  • Payroll mappings

Locked Payroll Requires Correction

An administrator must:

  1. Unlock the payroll period.
  2. Apply authorized corrections.
  3. Reapprove payroll.
  4. Lock the payroll period again.
  5. Generate a new export.

All actions are recorded in the audit log.
