Welcome to the Work-A-Beez Subprocessor Overview.

Work-A-Beez is committed to maintaining transparency regarding the third-party service providers ("subprocessors") that assist in delivering our services. This document explains how subprocessors are selected, the services they provide, and the safeguards implemented to protect customer data.

Purpose

Subprocessors enable Work-A-Beez to deliver reliable, secure, and scalable cloud services while allowing us to leverage specialized providers for infrastructure, communications, payments, monitoring, and customer support.

Every subprocessor is evaluated before being approved for use.

What is a Subprocessor?

A subprocessor is a third-party organization that processes customer data on behalf of Work-A-Beez to provide part of the service.

Examples include providers that offer:

  • Cloud hosting
  • Email delivery
  • Payment processing
  • Customer support

Subprocessors only receive the information necessary to perform their contracted services.

Why Work-A-Beez Uses Subprocessors

Using trusted service providers enables us to provide:

  • High availability
  • Reliable infrastructure
  • Secure communications
  • Payment processing
  • Performance monitoring
  • Customer support

All subprocessors are contractually obligated to protect customer information.

Subprocessor Categories

Work-A-Beez may use subprocessors in the following categories:

Cloud Infrastructure

Supports:

  • Application hosting
  • Compute resources
  • Databases
  • Storage
  • Networking

Email Delivery

Supports:

  • Password reset emails
  • Notifications
  • Account verification
  • Payroll report emails

Payment Processing

Supports:

  • Subscription billing
  • Payment collection
  • Invoice payments
  • Payment verification

Payment card information is processed directly by the payment provider and is not stored by Work-A-Beez unless explicitly stated.

Customer Support

Supports:

  • Support communications
  • Knowledge management
  • Help desk services

The table below lists representative categories of subprocessors used by Work-A-Beez. Specific vendors may change over time as part of our vendor management process.

Category Purpose Customer Data Processed

Email Provider Transactional emails Email addresses and notification content

A current list of named subprocessors is maintained by Work-A-Beez and may be provided to customers upon request or published through the Trust Center.

Data Residency

Customer data is processed and stored in accordance with the customer's selected hosting region and applicable legal or contractual requirements.

When subprocessors process customer data across geographic regions, appropriate safeguards are implemented to protect personal information and comply with applicable privacy regulations.

Security Requirements

Every approved subprocessor must satisfy Work-A-Beez security requirements.

Evaluation criteria include:

  • Information security practices
  • Encryption capabilities
  • Access controls
  • Incident response procedures
  • Business continuity planning
  • Compliance certifications (where applicable)
  • Privacy controls
  • Data handling procedures

Security reviews are conducted periodically based on vendor risk.

Vendor Risk Management

Before engaging a subprocessor, Work-A-Beez performs a risk assessment that may include:

  • Security questionnaire
  • Compliance review
  • Privacy assessment
  • Contract review
  • Business continuity evaluation
  • Financial stability review
  • Reputation assessment

Higher-risk vendors may require additional review and approval.

Data Processing Agreements

Work-A-Beez maintains appropriate contractual agreements with subprocessors that process customer data.

These agreements typically address:

  • Confidentiality
  • Security obligations
  • Data protection responsibilities
  • Privacy requirements
  • Breach notification obligations
  • Data deletion upon contract termination
  • Applicable regulatory requirements

Subprocessor Changes

Work-A-Beez periodically reviews and updates its list of subprocessors.

When material changes occur, customers may be notified in accordance with:

  • Applicable law
  • Contractual obligations
  • Data Processing Agreements (DPAs)
  • Subscription terms

Customers are encouraged to review updated subprocessor information as part of their vendor governance processes.

Customer Responsibilities

Customers should:

  • Review available subprocessor information.
  • Evaluate their own compliance requirements.
  • Execute applicable Data Processing Agreements if required.
  • Contact Work-A-Beez with vendor-related questions.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why doesn't Work-A-Beez build every service internally?

Using specialized providers allows Work-A-Beez to deliver secure, reliable, and scalable services while benefiting from vendors with deep expertise in infrastructure, communications, and payments.

Does every subprocessor receive all of my data?

No.

Each subprocessor receives only the minimum information necessary to perform its contracted function.

Are subprocessors contractually required to protect my data?

Yes.

All approved subprocessors must agree to contractual obligations regarding confidentiality, security, privacy, and applicable data protection requirements.

Can subprocessors access customer data?

Access is limited to authorized personnel only when necessary to provide contracted services and is governed by contractual, technical, and organizational safeguards.

Will I be notified if subprocessors change?

Where required by law, contractual commitments, or applicable agreements, Work-A-Beez will provide notice of material subprocessor changes.
