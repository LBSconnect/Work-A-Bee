Support & Escalation Guide

Welcome to the Work-A-Beez Support & Escalation Guide.

This guide explains how customers obtain technical assistance, submit support requests, monitor case progress, escalate issues, and work with the Work-A-Beez Customer Success team.

Our objective is to provide responsive, transparent, and effective support that minimizes disruption to your business.

Purpose

The Support & Escalation Guide defines the processes used to:

  • Request technical support
  • Report platform issues
  • Ask product questions
  • Escalate urgent incidents
  • Track issue resolution
  • Communicate service updates

This guide applies to all supported Work-A-Beez subscription plans.

Support Overview

The Work-A-Beez Customer Success team provides assistance with:

  • Account administration
  • User management
  • Login issues
  • Timekeeping
  • Scheduling
  • Leave management
  • Reporting
  • Payroll exports
  • Product guidance
  • Technical troubleshooting

Support requests are prioritized according to business impact.

Support Channels

Customers may contact support through:

Email Support

Appropriate for:

  • Technical issues
  • General inquiries
  • Non-urgent issues
  • Documentation requests
  • Configuration questions

Currently, email is the primary support channel for Work-A-Beez customers.

Support Hours

Support availability depends on the customer's subscription agreement.

Typical support availability includes:

  • Standard Business Hours
  • Extended Business Hours (where contracted)

Emergency production incidents may receive priority attention outside standard business hours according to the applicable service agreement.

Before Contacting Support

To help resolve issues more quickly, gather the following information before submitting a request:

  • Organization name
  • Administrator name
  • User experiencing the issue
  • Description of the problem
  • Date and time the issue occurred
  • Steps to reproduce
  • Error messages
  • Screenshots (if applicable)
  • Browser and operating system

Providing complete information helps reduce resolution time.

Creating a Support Request

A quality support request should include:

Summary

A brief description of the issue.

Example:

Employees unable to clock in from the office computer.

Business Impact

Explain how the issue affects daily operations.

Expected Behavior

Describe what should have occurred.

Actual Behavior

Describe what actually occurred.

Supporting Evidence

Attach screenshots or other relevant information whenever available.

Ticket Lifecycle

Support requests progress through the following stages:

  1. Submitted
  2. Acknowledged
  3. Assigned
  4. Investigation
  5. Resolution in Progress
  6. Customer Validation
  7. Closed

Customers receive status updates throughout the lifecycle of the ticket.

Ticket Priorities

Support tickets are assigned a priority based on business impact.

Critical

Examples:

  • Complete production outage
  • Login unavailable
  • Payroll processing blocked
  • Security incident

Immediate attention is provided.

High

Examples:

  • Major feature unavailable
  • Multiple users affected

High-priority issues receive accelerated investigation.

Medium

Examples:

  • Individual feature malfunction
  • Reporting issue
  • Performance degradation affecting limited users

Operations continue with workarounds where available.

Low

Examples:

  • Cosmetic issue
  • Minor usability concern
  • Documentation clarification
  • Feature request

These issues are addressed according to product planning and operational priorities.

Escalation Process

If additional attention is required, customers may request escalation.

Typical escalation path:

  1. Customer Support Representative
  2. Technical Support Lead
  3. Engineering Team
  4. Engineering Manager
  5. Executive Leadership (when appropriate)

Escalation is based on business impact rather than the age of the ticket alone.

Communication During an Incident

For significant service disruptions, Work-A-Beez provides regular updates regarding:

  • Current status
  • Services affected
  • Estimated restoration time (when available)
  • Temporary workarounds
  • Resolution progress

Communications are delivered by email.

Customer Responsibilities

Customers can help expedite issue resolution by:

  • Providing accurate information
  • Responding promptly to requests for additional details
  • Testing proposed solutions
  • Maintaining supported browsers and devices
  • Assigning an administrator as the primary support contact

Customer cooperation is often essential for effective troubleshooting.

Administrator Responsibilities

Organization administrators are responsible for:

  • Managing user accounts
  • Confirming reported issues
  • Coordinating with affected users
  • Implementing recommended configuration changes
  • Communicating internally with employees when necessary

Administrators serve as the primary point of contact between the organization and Work-A-Beez Support.

Case Closure

A support case may be closed when:

  • The issue has been resolved.
  • The requested information has been provided.
  • The customer confirms successful resolution.
  • No response is received after reasonable follow-up attempts.
  • The issue is determined to be outside the scope of Work-A-Beez support.

Closed cases may be reopened if additional related information becomes available.

Feedback & Satisfaction

Customer feedback helps improve support services.

After a case is resolved, customers may be invited to provide feedback regarding:

  • Response time
  • Resolution quality
  • Communication
  • Professionalism
  • Overall satisfaction

Feedback is reviewed regularly as part of Work-A-Beez's continuous improvement process.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I reopen a closed ticket?

Yes. If the issue recurs or additional information becomes available, you may request that the ticket be reopened or submit a new request referencing the previous case.

How are enhancement requests handled?

Enhancement requests are reviewed by the Product Management team and evaluated based on customer value, strategic priorities, and technical feasibility. Submission does not guarantee implementation.

Will I receive updates while my issue is being investigated?

Yes. Work-A-Beez provides periodic updates for active support cases, particularly those involving high-priority or customer-impacting issues.
