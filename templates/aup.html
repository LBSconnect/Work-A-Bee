Work-A-Beez Acceptable Use Policy (AUP)

Effective Date: July 23, 2026

This Acceptable Use Policy ("AUP") governs access to and use of the Work-A-Beez Software-as-a-Service ("Service") provided by Linton Business Solutions, LLC ("LBS").

This AUP forms part of the Work-A-Beez Terms of Service and applies to every Customer, Administrator, Employee User, Contractor, and any individual accessing the Service through a Customer account.

Failure to comply with this AUP may result in suspension or termination of access to the Service.

1. Purpose

The purpose of this Acceptable Use Policy is to:

  • Protect the security, availability, and integrity of the Work-A-Beez platform.
  • Promote responsible and lawful use of the Service.
  • Protect Customer Data and the data of other customers.
  • Prevent fraud, abuse, and unauthorized access.
  • Maintain reliable service for all customers.
  • Define activities that are prohibited while using Work-A-Beez.

This AUP supplements, but does not replace, the Work-A-Beez Terms of Service.

2. Scope

This AUP applies to all use of:

  • Work-A-Beez Web Application
  • Administrative Dashboard
  • Employee Portal
  • APIs
  • Reports
  • Notifications
  • Integrations
  • Trial Accounts
  • Paid Accounts
  • Future Work-A-Beez Services

Every user accessing Work-A-Beez is responsible for complying with this Policy.

3. Authorized Use

Customers may use Work-A-Beez solely for legitimate business purposes related to workforce management, including:

  • Employee timekeeping
  • Employee scheduling
  • Attendance management
  • PTO management
  • Payroll reporting
  • Workforce administration
  • Internal business communications
  • Reporting and analytics
  • Management oversight

The Service may not be used for unlawful, deceptive, or unauthorized purposes.

4. Customer Responsibilities

Each Customer is responsible for:

  • Maintaining accurate account information.
  • Managing authorized users.
  • Protecting login credentials.
  • Removing inactive users.
  • Reviewing employee permissions.
  • Maintaining appropriate internal security controls.
  • Ensuring employees comply with this Policy.
  • Verifying payroll information before processing payroll.

Customers remain responsible for all activity occurring under their accounts unless caused solely by LBS.

5. User Responsibilities

Every authorized user agrees to:

  • Use the Service only for authorized business purposes.
  • Protect usernames and passwords.
  • Report suspected unauthorized access immediately.
  • Keep account information accurate.
  • Use the Service honestly and professionally.
  • Follow applicable laws and employer policies.
  • Respect the privacy of other users.

Users may not share login credentials with unauthorized individuals.

6. Account Security

Customers must use reasonable efforts to protect access to Work-A-Beez. Recommended security practices include:

  • Strong passwords
  • Unique passwords
  • Multi-factor authentication where available
  • Secure company-managed devices
  • Timely software updates
  • Antivirus or endpoint protection
  • Prompt removal of terminated employees

LBS reserves the right to temporarily restrict access to accounts that appear compromised until account ownership can be verified.

7. Acceptable Business Uses

The following activities are generally permitted:

  • Recording employee work hours
  • Managing employee schedules
  • Approving timecards
  • Running payroll reports
  • Managing departments
  • Tracking attendance
  • Managing PTO requests
  • Creating workforce reports
  • Reviewing productivity metrics
  • Maintaining employee records related to workforce management

Use of the Service must always remain consistent with applicable employment, labor, payroll, and privacy laws.

8. Customer Data Ownership

Customer Data entered into Work-A-Beez remains the property of the Customer. LBS receives only the limited rights necessary to:

  • Host the Service;
  • Process Customer Data;
  • Secure the platform;
  • Provide customer support;
  • Improve platform reliability;
  • Fulfill contractual obligations.

LBS does not claim ownership of Customer business information or employee records.

9. Privacy Expectations

Users should access only information necessary to perform their assigned job responsibilities. Customers are responsible for establishing internal privacy policies governing employee access to workforce information. LBS provides access controls, but Customers remain responsible for configuring permissions appropriately.

10. Responsible Administration

Administrative users possess elevated privileges and therefore assume additional responsibilities. Administrators should:

  • Regularly review user permissions.
  • Remove unnecessary administrative access.
  • Verify payroll reports.
  • Monitor unusual account activity.
  • Secure administrator credentials.
  • Review audit logs where available.
  • Promptly disable former employee accounts.

Improper administrative use may result in suspension of administrative privileges.

11. Compliance with Laws

Customers agree to use Work-A-Beez in compliance with all applicable laws, regulations, and governmental requirements, including those relating to:

  • Employment practices
  • Wage and hour laws
  • Payroll obligations
  • Tax reporting
  • Privacy and data protection
  • Anti-discrimination laws
  • Electronic communications
  • Record retention

LBS does not provide legal, payroll, tax, or human resources advice. Customers remain solely responsible for ensuring that their use of the Service complies with applicable legal requirements.

12. Prohibited Activities

Users may not use Work-A-Beez to engage in any activity that is unlawful, fraudulent, harmful, or that interferes with the security, integrity, or availability of the Service. Without limitation, Customers and Users shall not:

  • Violate applicable laws or regulations;
  • Infringe the intellectual property rights of others;
  • Attempt unauthorized access to any account, system, or network;
  • Circumvent authentication or security controls;
  • Misrepresent identity or organizational affiliation;
  • Use the Service to facilitate fraud, identity theft, or other criminal activity;
  • Interfere with another customer's use of the Service.

13. Unauthorized Access

Users shall not attempt to:

  • Access accounts belonging to another customer;
  • Access data for which they lack authorization;
  • Elevate privileges without authorization;
  • Exploit software vulnerabilities;
  • Bypass permission controls;
  • Intercept network traffic belonging to others;
  • Access administrative functions without authorization.

Any attempt to gain unauthorized access may result in immediate suspension pending investigation.

14. Malware and Malicious Code

Customers and Users may not upload, transmit, distribute, or execute viruses, worms, Trojan horses, ransomware, spyware, keyloggers, cryptomining software, malicious scripts, logic bombs, or any software intended to disrupt or compromise systems.

LBS reserves the right to remove or quarantine content reasonably believed to contain malicious code.

15. Reverse Engineering and Platform Exploitation

Except where expressly permitted by applicable law, Users shall not:

  • Reverse engineer the Service;
  • Decompile or disassemble software;
  • Attempt to discover source code;
  • Circumvent licensing mechanisms;
  • Bypass subscription restrictions;
  • Remove proprietary notices;
  • Create derivative works based on the Service;
  • Attempt to replicate or copy platform functionality for competitive purposes.

Nothing in this section limits rights that cannot be waived under applicable law.

16. Security Testing

Customers may not perform penetration testing, vulnerability scanning, stress testing, or other security testing against the Work-A-Beez platform without prior written authorization from LBS. Authorized security testing may be coordinated through the LBS security contact and must follow mutually agreed rules of engagement.

17. API Misuse

Where APIs are provided, Users shall not:

  • Exceed documented rate limits;
  • Circumvent authentication mechanisms;
  • Harvest data unrelated to authorized business purposes;
  • Use automated tools to overload API endpoints;
  • Attempt to discover undocumented interfaces through abusive means;
  • Interfere with API availability for other customers.

LBS may implement rate limiting, throttling, or temporary blocking to protect platform stability.

18. Automated Access and Scraping

Except where expressly authorized in writing, Users may not:

  • Scrape the Service;
  • Use bots to collect platform content;
  • Automate login attempts;
  • Harvest customer information;
  • Use robotic processes to copy proprietary interface elements;
  • Extract datasets for resale;
  • Mirror or reproduce substantial portions of the Service.

Reasonable automation using officially supported integrations or APIs is permitted where authorized.

19. Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning Restrictions

Customers may use AI tools with data they lawfully control; however, they may not use Work-A-Beez to:

  • Train public AI models using data belonging to other customers;
  • Extract proprietary software functionality for AI model training;
  • Circumvent licensing restrictions through automated AI systems;
  • Generate unauthorized copies of the platform or its documentation.

Nothing in this Policy prohibits Customers from using their own exported data with AI tools, provided such use complies with applicable law and the Customer's obligations to its employees and third parties.

20. Intellectual Property Protection

Users shall not:

  • Copy substantial portions of the Service;
  • Remove copyright, trademark, or proprietary notices;
  • Misrepresent ownership of Work-A-Beez technology;
  • Distribute proprietary documentation without authorization;
  • Use Work-A-Beez branding in a misleading manner.

All software, source code, interfaces, designs, trademarks, logos, and related intellectual property remain the exclusive property of Linton Business Solutions, LLC or its licensors.

21. Spam, Phishing, and Deceptive Practices

The Service may not be used to send unsolicited bulk communications, conduct phishing campaigns, impersonate another individual or organization, distribute fraudulent communications, mislead employees or third parties, or collect credentials through deceptive means.

LBS may suspend accounts engaged in such activities without prior notice where necessary to protect the Service or affected parties.

22. Excessive Resource Consumption

Users may not intentionally or negligently consume system resources in a manner that materially degrades the Service for others. Examples include:

  • Automated requests designed to overwhelm systems;
  • Excessive API traffic;
  • Repeated large-scale exports without legitimate business need;
  • Deliberate denial-of-service activity;
  • Automated account creation;
  • Resource abuse inconsistent with normal platform usage.

LBS may temporarily limit or suspend resource-intensive activity to preserve service availability.

23. Fraudulent Activity

Customers and Users may not use Work-A-Beez to falsify time records, manipulate attendance records, misrepresent payroll information, conceal unauthorized activity, facilitate financial fraud, circumvent employment controls, or commit identity fraud.

Customers remain responsible for implementing internal approval processes and verifying payroll accuracy.

24. Interference with Platform Operations

Users shall not intentionally interfere with the operation of the Service by attempting to disable infrastructure, circumventing security controls, introducing malicious workloads, exploiting software defects, interfering with monitoring systems, or disrupting customer environments.

LBS reserves the right to immediately isolate activity that poses a material risk to platform stability.

25. Protection of Other Customers

Customers acknowledge that Work-A-Beez is a multi-tenant SaaS platform. Accordingly, no Customer may engage in conduct that compromises another customer's confidentiality, attempts cross-tenant access, interferes with another customer's operations, degrades shared platform performance, or circumvents tenant isolation mechanisms.

Protection of all customer environments is a core security objective of Work-A-Beez.

26. Monitoring and Enforcement

To protect the security, integrity, and availability of the Work-A-Beez platform, Linton Business Solutions, LLC ("LBS") may monitor platform operations, system performance, and security-related events. Such monitoring is performed for legitimate business purposes, including:

  • Detecting security threats;
  • Preventing fraud;
  • Investigating abuse;
  • Maintaining platform stability;
  • Troubleshooting technical issues;
  • Complying with legal obligations.

LBS does not routinely monitor the content of Customer Data except as necessary to provide the Service, respond to support requests, investigate suspected violations, or comply with applicable law.

27. Investigation of Suspected Violations

LBS reserves the right to investigate activity that reasonably appears to violate this AUP, the Terms of Service, applicable law, or the security of the platform. Investigations may include:

  • Reviewing relevant system logs;
  • Reviewing authentication records;
  • Reviewing audit trails;
  • Examining network activity;
  • Consulting affected Customers;
  • Preserving evidence necessary for legal or security purposes.

Customers agree to cooperate reasonably with investigations involving their accounts.

28. Temporary Suspension

LBS may suspend all or part of a Customer's access to the Service without prior notice when reasonably necessary to:

  • Protect platform security;
  • Prevent unauthorized access;
  • Stop ongoing abuse;
  • Mitigate a Security Incident;
  • Comply with applicable law;
  • Protect other Customers;
  • Protect LBS personnel or infrastructure.

Where practical, LBS will provide notice of the suspension and the reason for it. Suspension is intended to be temporary and limited to what is reasonably necessary to address the issue.

29. Termination for Cause

LBS may terminate the Agreement and permanently disable access to the Service if a Customer or User:

  • Materially violates this AUP;
  • Repeatedly violates this AUP after notice;
  • Uses the Service for unlawful purposes;
  • Engages in fraud;
  • Attempts unauthorized access to other customer environments;
  • Intentionally disrupts the Service;
  • Uses the Service in a manner that creates significant legal, operational, or security risk.

Termination under this section does not limit any other rights or remedies available to LBS under the Agreement or applicable law.

30. Preservation of Evidence

LBS may preserve relevant records, logs, and system information where reasonably necessary to investigate violations, respond to litigation, comply with legal obligations, cooperate with law enforcement, or protect the rights of LBS, Customers, or third parties.

Preservation of information does not imply wrongdoing by any party.

31. Reporting Violations

Customers and Users are encouraged to promptly report suspected violations of this AUP, including:

  • Unauthorized account access;
  • Suspected data breaches;
  • Malware activity;
  • Fraudulent use of the Service;
  • Abuse of platform resources;
  • Intellectual property violations;
  • Security vulnerabilities.

Reports should include sufficient detail to allow investigation where possible.

32. Export Controls and Sanctions

Customers agree not to use the Service in violation of applicable export control laws, economic sanctions, or trade restrictions. Customers represent that they are not prohibited from using the Service under applicable United States laws or regulations. LBS reserves the right to restrict or terminate access where required by law.

33. Compliance with Third-Party Terms

Certain Work-A-Beez features rely on third-party service providers. Customers agree to comply with applicable third-party terms when using integrated services, including but not limited to payment processors, email delivery services, cloud infrastructure providers, authentication providers, and communication services.

Nothing in this AUP modifies the contractual relationship between Customers and those third-party providers.

34. No Waiver

Failure by LBS to enforce any provision of this AUP shall not constitute a waiver of its right to enforce that provision or any other provision at a later time.

35. Policy Updates

LBS may modify this Acceptable Use Policy from time to time to address evolving security threats, reflect changes in applicable law, support new platform functionality, improve platform security, or clarify acceptable practices. Material updates will become effective in accordance with the notice provisions contained in the Work-A-Beez Terms of Service.

Continued use of the Service after the effective date of an updated AUP constitutes acceptance of the revised Policy, unless otherwise prohibited by applicable law.

36. Severability

If any provision of this AUP is determined to be invalid or unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall remain in full force and effect. The invalid provision shall be interpreted or modified only to the extent necessary to make it enforceable while preserving its intended purpose.

37. Governing Law

This Acceptable Use Policy shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the State of Texas, without regard to its conflict of law principles. Any dispute arising under this AUP shall be resolved in accordance with the dispute resolution provisions contained in the Work-A-Beez Terms of Service.
