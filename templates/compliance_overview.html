Work-A-Beez Compliance Overview

Last updated: {{ updated.strftime('%B %d, %Y') }}

Work-A-Beez is committed to operating a secure, reliable, and trustworthy workforce management platform that helps customers meet their own organizational compliance obligations.

Compliance is an ongoing process involving security, privacy, governance, risk management, operational controls, and continuous improvement.

This document provides an overview of the compliance practices that support the Work-A-Beez platform.

Important: This document is provided for informational purposes only and should not be interpreted as legal advice. Customers remain responsible for understanding and complying with the laws and regulations applicable to their organization.

Compliance Philosophy

Compliance is integrated into every stage of the Work-A-Beez platform lifecycle.

Our objectives are to:

  • Protect customer information
  • Support regulatory requirements
  • Reduce organizational risk
  • Maintain customer trust
  • Continuously improve operational practices

Compliance activities include:

  • Security reviews
  • Privacy assessments
  • Policy management
  • Internal process reviews
  • Documentation updates
  • Operational monitoring

Shared Responsibility Model

Maintaining compliance is a shared responsibility between Work-A-Beez and our customers.

Work-A-Beez Responsibilities

Work-A-Beez is responsible for:

  • Maintaining platform security
  • Protecting hosted infrastructure
  • Providing access controls
  • Operating secure authentication
  • Maintaining platform availability
  • Protecting customer data
  • Maintaining operational policies

Customer Responsibilities

Customers are responsible for:

  • Configuring company settings
  • Assigning user permissions
  • Maintaining employee records
  • Complying with labor laws
  • Reviewing payroll calculations
  • Managing company policies
  • Monitoring user activity
  • Configuring PTO policies
  • Managing overtime rules
  • Maintaining legal employment records

Regulatory Considerations

Organizations using Work-A-Beez may be subject to laws and regulations based upon their location, industry, and business activities.

Examples include:

  • Wage and Hour Laws
  • Fair Labor Standards
  • Employment Record Requirements
  • Privacy Regulations
  • Tax Reporting Requirements
  • Industry-Specific Regulations

Work-A-Beez provides tools that may assist organizations in meeting compliance obligations but does not guarantee compliance with any specific law or regulation.

Customers should consult qualified legal or regulatory professionals when necessary.

Privacy Program

Protecting personal information is a core element of our compliance program.

Privacy practices include:

  • Data minimization
  • Secure processing
  • Controlled access
  • Encryption
  • Customer transparency
  • User rights support
  • Data retention controls

Additional details are available in the Privacy Policy and Data Protection Overview.

Security Governance

Security governance includes documented policies and operational procedures designed to protect the platform.

Examples include:

  • Access Management
  • Password Management
  • Authentication Controls
  • Change Management
  • Incident Response
  • Vulnerability Management
  • Backup Procedures
  • Disaster Recovery

These controls are regularly reviewed and updated.

Data Protection

Customer information is protected through administrative, technical, and organizational safeguards.

Examples include:

  • Encryption
  • Role-Based Access Control
  • Secure Authentication
  • Audit Logging
  • Monitoring
  • Backup Procedures

Additional information is available within the Data Protection Overview.

Workforce Compliance Features

Work-A-Beez includes features designed to assist organizations with workforce administration.

Examples include:

Timekeeping

  • Accurate time tracking
  • Break recording
  • Meal period tracking
  • Overtime calculations
  • Attendance history

Leave Management

Support for:

  • Vacation
  • Sick Leave
  • Personal Leave
  • Jury Duty
  • Military Leave
  • Bereavement
  • Family Leave
  • Custom Leave Policies

Scheduling

Organizations can:

  • Publish schedules
  • Track schedule changes
  • Document shift assignments
  • Monitor staffing coverage

Employee Records

Administrators can securely maintain:

  • Employee profiles
  • Employment history
  • Departments
  • Job titles
  • Contact information
  • Company documents

Audit Logging

Certain administrative actions are recorded to assist organizations with operational oversight.

Examples include:

  • User creation
  • User removal
  • Permission changes
  • Password resets
  • Schedule modifications
  • Timecard edits
  • PTO approvals
  • Administrative configuration changes

Audit records help organizations review historical activity and investigate operational issues.

Access Controls

Work-A-Beez uses Role-Based Access Control (RBAC) to limit access to authorized users.

Examples of supported roles include:

  • System Administrator
  • Company Administrator
  • HR Administrator
  • Payroll Administrator
  • Manager
  • Supervisor
  • Employee
  • Auditor

Organizations are encouraged to periodically review user permissions.

Risk Management

Risk management activities include:

  • Operational risk assessments
  • Security evaluations
  • Software testing
  • Dependency reviews
  • Infrastructure monitoring
  • Vendor reviews
  • Incident analysis

Risk management is an ongoing process rather than a one-time activity.

Vendor Management

Work-A-Beez may utilize carefully selected third-party service providers to support platform operations.

Potential providers may include:

  • Cloud Infrastructure
  • Payment Processing
  • Email Delivery
  • Monitoring
  • Analytics
  • Customer Support

Service providers are evaluated based on operational and security considerations.

Additional details are available within the Subprocessor Overview.

Incident Management

Work-A-Beez maintains documented procedures for responding to operational and security incidents.

Typical phases include:

  1. Detection
  2. Assessment
  3. Investigation
  4. Containment
  5. Recovery
  6. Root Cause Analysis
  7. Continuous Improvement

Where required, customers may be notified of confirmed incidents affecting customer information.

Data Retention

Customer information is retained only as long as necessary to:

  • Deliver requested services
  • Meet contractual obligations
  • Comply with legal requirements
  • Resolve disputes
  • Protect legitimate business interests

Customers may review additional information within the Data Retention Policy.

Business Continuity

Work-A-Beez maintains business continuity practices designed to minimize service interruptions.

These include:

  • Redundant infrastructure
  • Backup procedures
  • Disaster recovery planning
  • Monitoring
  • Operational documentation
  • Incident response procedures

Business continuity planning is reviewed periodically.

Continuous Improvement

Compliance is continuously evaluated through:

  • Policy reviews
  • Security improvements
  • Software updates
  • Customer feedback
  • Operational assessments
  • Documentation updates
  • Internal reviews

As regulations evolve, Work-A-Beez may update policies and procedures accordingly.

Customer Best Practices

Customers are encouraged to:

  • Enable Multi-Factor Authentication
  • Review permissions regularly
  • Remove inactive users
  • Train employees on cybersecurity
  • Review audit logs
  • Maintain accurate employee records
  • Regularly update company policies
  • Keep browsers and devices current

Strong compliance depends on both technology and organizational practices.
