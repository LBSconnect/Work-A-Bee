The Work-A-Beez Status Page provides real-time visibility into the operational status of our cloud services. Customers can monitor platform health, view scheduled maintenance, receive incident updates, and subscribe to service notifications.

The status page promotes transparency and helps customers stay informed about service availability and operational events.

Overview

The Work-A-Beez Status Page provides near real-time information regarding the health and availability of platform services.

Customers can use the Status Page to:

  • Monitor service availability
  • View ongoing incidents
  • Review scheduled maintenance
  • Subscribe to notifications
  • Access historical uptime information
  • Track incident progress

The Status Page is available independently of the main Work-A-Beez application whenever possible.

Purpose

The Status Page is intended to provide transparent communication regarding platform availability.

It helps customers:

  • Determine whether an issue is isolated or system-wide
  • Monitor maintenance activities
  • Stay informed during service interruptions
  • Reduce unnecessary support requests
  • Plan around scheduled maintenance

Service Components

The Status Page displays the operational status of major platform components.

Typical components include:

  • Authentication Services
  • Web Application
  • Scheduling
  • Time & Attendance
  • Leave Management
  • Payroll Services
  • Reporting & Analytics
  • Notifications
  • File Import & Export Services

Each component is monitored independently.

Service Status Indicators

Each service component displays one of the following statuses.

Status Description
Operational Service is functioning normally.
Degraded Performance Service is available but performance may be slower than expected.
Partial Outage Some functionality or users are affected.
Major Outage Significant disruption impacting one or more core services.
Maintenance Planned maintenance is currently in progress.

Status indicators are updated as new information becomes available.

Planned Maintenance

Routine maintenance helps maintain security, reliability, and performance.

Planned maintenance may include:

  • Infrastructure upgrades
  • Security updates
  • Database maintenance
  • Network improvements
  • Platform enhancements

Whenever practical, maintenance is scheduled during periods of lower customer activity.

Customers are notified in advance of planned maintenance that may impact service availability.

Incident Management

When an unexpected service disruption occurs, the Status Page provides timely updates throughout the incident.

Incident communications typically include:

  • Summary of the issue
  • Affected services
  • Impact assessment
  • Current investigation status
  • Mitigation efforts
  • Estimated next update
  • Resolution confirmation

Updates are published as additional information becomes available.

Incident Severity Levels

Incidents are classified to help communicate customer impact.

Severity 1 - Critical

Examples:

  • Complete platform outage
  • Authentication unavailable
  • Widespread customer impact

Immediate response is initiated.

Severity 2 - High

Examples:

  • Major feature unavailable
  • Significant degradation
  • Large customer impact

Engineering teams begin investigation immediately.

Severity 3 - Medium

Examples:

  • Limited feature disruption
  • Performance issues
  • Moderate customer impact

Resolution is prioritized according to business impact.

Severity 4 - Low

Examples:

  • Cosmetic issues
  • Minor usability concerns
  • Isolated defects

Resolution is scheduled through normal maintenance and release processes.

Incident Lifecycle

Incidents generally progress through the following stages.

  1. Detection
  2. Investigation
  3. Identification
  4. Mitigation
  5. Resolution
  6. Monitoring
  7. Post-Incident Review

Customers receive updates during significant milestones whenever practical.

Historical Service Status

The Status Page maintains a history of previous service events, allowing customers to review:

  • Past incidents
  • Planned maintenance
  • Historical uptime
  • Resolution timelines
  • Service availability trends

Historical information supports operational reviews and customer reporting.

Notification Subscriptions

Customers may subscribe to receive service updates through supported channels.

Available subscription methods may include:

  • Email notifications
  • RSS feed

Subscribers receive updates for:

  • New incidents
  • Incident status changes
  • Incident resolution
  • Scheduled maintenance
  • Maintenance completion

Notification options may vary by subscription plan or deployment.

Customer Communication

During significant service events, Work-A-Beez aims to communicate:

  • What happened
  • Which services are affected
  • Current customer impact
  • Actions being taken
  • Expected resolution timeline (when available)
  • Recommended customer actions

Updates continue until the incident is resolved and service stability has been confirmed.

Frequently Asked Questions

How often is the Status Page updated?

Updates are published as meaningful new information becomes available throughout an incident or maintenance event.

Does an Operational status guarantee no issues?

No.

An Operational status indicates that platform monitoring has not detected widespread service disruption. Individual user issues may still occur due to local network conditions, browser configuration, or organization-specific environments.

Should I contact Support if the Status Page shows an outage?

You generally do not need to report an issue already identified on the Status Page unless your organization is experiencing additional problems not reflected in the published incident.

What if my issue is not listed?

If you believe you are experiencing a platform issue that is not reflected on the Status Page, contact the Work-A-Beez Support Center for assistance.
