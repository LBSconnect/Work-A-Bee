Who this policy covers

Work-A-Bee is provided by [Your Company Legal Name] ("we", "us") to businesses ("Company", "you") for employee time tracking and payroll. Your Company is the controller of its own employee data — it decides what to collect and why — and we act as a processor, handling that data only to provide the service and only as your Company's admins direct. If you're an employee using Work-A-Bee at your employer's direction, your employer's own policies govern how your information is used; contact your employer with questions about your employee data.

What we collect

  • Company information: company name and code, address, phone, website, timezone, payroll settings, and the email addresses you designate to receive reports.
  • Admin accounts: name, username, email, job title, and a securely hashed password (we never store your password in plain text).
  • Employee records: name, employee ID, job title, hourly rate, and a securely hashed PIN (we never store PINs in plain text).
  • Time & attendance data: clock-in and clock-out timestamps, and which admin made any manual correction.
  • Device & security data: a name you give each authorized device, a securely hashed device token, and the IP address last used to clock in, so we can restrict clock-ins to computers your Company has approved.

We do not collect biometric data of any kind — no fingerprint, face, or voice scans are used to clock employees in or out.

How we use it

We use this data only to operate the service: authenticating admins and employees, tracking hours, calculating pay, generating the timesheets and payroll reports your Company requests, enforcing which devices are authorized to clock in and out, and maintaining an audit trail of admin actions like manual time entries and device changes.

Who we share it with — and who we don't sell it to

We do not sell or rent personal data to anyone, for any purpose. Weekly payroll reports are sent only to the recipients your Company configures, using Microsoft's email sending service (Microsoft Graph API) as our email delivery provider. We don't share your data with any other third party except as required by law (for example, a valid legal request).

Data isolation between companies

Each Company's data is logically separated in our database and is only accessible to that Company's own admins and employees — never to other companies using Work-A-Bee.

Security

Passwords, employee PINs, and device tokens are stored as one-way cryptographic hashes, never in plain text. Login is rate-limited to reduce brute-force attempts, and administrative actions like manual time entries and device registration are logged. No method of storage or transmission is 100% secure, but we take reasonable technical measures to protect your data.

Cookies

We use a session cookie to keep you logged in and a device-authorization cookie to remember which office computers your Company has approved for clock-ins. Both are strictly functional and required for the service to work — we don't use advertising or cross-site tracking cookies.

Data retention & deletion

We retain your Company's data for as long as your account is active, so timesheets and payroll history remain available to you. If you'd like your Company's data deleted, contact us at info@lbsconnect.net and we'll process the request within a reasonable time, except where we're required to keep records for legal reasons.

International transfers

Work-A-Bee's infrastructure currently runs in the United States. If that changes, this policy will be updated to describe any cross-border data transfers.

Children's privacy

Work-A-Bee is a business tool intended for use by employers and their adult workforce. It is not directed at, and we do not knowingly collect data from, children.

Changes to this policy

We may update this policy as the product changes. We'll update the "Last updated" date above when we do.

Contact

Questions about this policy, or want to exercise a data access/deletion request? Email info@lbsconnect.net.
